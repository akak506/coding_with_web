Foods That Fight PCOD

Fatty Fish


Fatty fish like salmon is beneficial for PCOS due to its omega-3 fatty acids, which help reduce inflammation, regulate insulin levels, and improve hormone balance, mitigating some symptoms of PCOS.

Leafy Greens


Leafy greens like spinach and kale are rich in antioxidants, such as vitamins C and E. These antioxidants help combat inflammation, a common issue in PCOS. Additionally, they're high in fiber, which aids in blood sugar regulation and insulin control, critical for PCOS management.

Eggs


Eggs are a valuable source of high-quality protein, which supports muscle development and hormonal balance. Protein helps regulate appetite, potentially aiding weight managementâ€”a crucial aspect of PCOS care. The amino acids in eggs contribute to hormone production and function, important for managing PCOS symptoms.

Fresh Fruits


Fruits provide essential vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber. This combination is effective in improving insulin resistance, a core challenge in PCOS. Some fruits, like berries and apples, have a lower glycemic index, preventing rapid blood sugar spikes. Antioxidants found in fruits combat oxidative stress and inflammation, supporting overall health and symptom alleviation.

Whole Grains


Whole grains like oats, quinoa, and brown rice are complex carbohydrates with a lower glycemic index. They release glucose gradually, preventing sudden insulin surges. Consumption of whole grains can enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes, often linked to PCOS. The fiber in whole grains aids digestion and helps with weight management.

Cinnamon


Cinnamon contains bioactive compounds that can improve insulin sensitivity. Cinnamaldehyde, a key component, helps regulate blood sugar levels by mimicking insulin's action. Incorporating cinnamon into your diet may assist in better blood sugar control, a vital aspect of PCOS management.
